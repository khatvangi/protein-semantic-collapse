CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. PHOSPHORYLATION NEAR D/E:
   SUPPORTED - S/T/Y residues within 3 positions of D/E show 1.23x
   higher phosphorylation rate (p = 3.36e-02)
   This supports D/E regions as kinase recognition motifs.

2. BIOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION:
   - D/E residues themselves cannot be modified by common PTMs
   - However, D/E-rich regions serve as REGULATORY HOTSPOTS by:
     a) Providing acidic flanking sequences for kinase recognition
     b) Creating negative charge patches for protein interactions
     c) Potentially mimicking phosphorylation (phospho-mimicry)

3. IMPLICATIONS FOR RNA-BINDING PROTEINS:
   - D/E regions in RBDs may modulate RNA binding affinity
   - Phosphorylation near D/E could switch RBP activity
   - Charge-based regulation in disordered regions
