# Literature-Verified RBD Family Evolutionary Origins

**Date:** 2026-02-01

## Verified Classifications

| Family | Origin | Evidence | Source |
|--------|--------|----------|--------|
| **KH** | LUCA | "conservation in Bacteria, Archaea and Eukaryotes suggests KH arose early in evolution" | [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KH_domain), [InterPro](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/interpro/entry/interpro/IPR004087) |
| **LSM/Sm/Hfq** | LUCA | "Sm proteins exist in all three domains of life" - Hfq (bacteria), Lsm (archaea), Sm (eukaryotes) | [PMC3710371](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3710371/) |
| **DEAD-box** | LUCA | "present in eukaryotes, archaea and bacteria" - 1,848 bacterial DEAD-box proteins identified | [PMC3093544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3093544/) |
| **dsRBD** | LUCA | "found in eukaryotic, prokaryotic and viral proteins" - E.coli RNase III is classic example | [Nature Reviews](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrm1528) |
| **RRM** | Primarily Eukaryotic | "most abundant protein domain in eukaryotes" but "also present in bacteria" (rare, mainly cyanobacteria) | [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_recognition_motif) |
| **S1** | LUCA | Ribosomal protein, universal in all three domains | Standard biochemistry |
| **zf-C2H2** | Eukaryotic | Classic zinc fingers are eukaryotic | Standard knowledge |
| **zf-CCCH** | Eukaryotic | CCCH zinc fingers primarily eukaryotic | Standard knowledge |
| **zf-CCHC** | Eukaryotic/Viral | Retroviral nucleocapsid zinc fingers | Standard knowledge |

## Key Corrections to Original Classifications

| Family | Original | Corrected | Reason |
|--------|----------|-----------|--------|
| **DEAD** | Eukaryotic | **LUCA** | Literature clearly shows bacterial DEAD-box proteins |
| **dsRBD** | Eukaryotic | **LUCA** | E.coli RNase III has dsRBD |
| **Helicase_C** | Eukaryotic | **LUCA** | Part of DEAD-box/helicase superfamily |
| **RRM** | Eukaryotic | **Primarily Eukaryotic** | Rare in bacteria but present |

## Summary

**LUCA Origin (Ancient):**
- KH, LSM, DEAD, dsRBD, Helicase_C, S1, (RRM rare)

**Eukaryotic Innovation:**
- RRM (major expansion), zf-C2H2, zf-CCCH, zf-CCHC, PUF

## Implications

This dramatically changes our interpretation:
- Most RBD families are ANCIENT (LUCA origin)
- RRM is the major EUKARYOTIC innovation/expansion
- D/E autoinhibition is NOT correlated with eukaryotic vs ancient origin
